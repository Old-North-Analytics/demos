How to Use This Tool

Start by clicking "Load Sample Data" in the top-right corner. This populates all tabs with 20 jobs, 16 employees across five crews, and pre-set assignments so you have a working starting point.

On the Schedule tab, use the day buttons at the top to navigate between the 10 business days (April 7-18, 2026). Each day shows which jobs are scheduled and which employees are available.

To assign an employee to a job, drag their chip from the left panel and drop it onto the job's drop zone on the right. You can also use the day's dropdown list below each crew group as an alternative to drag-and-drop.

To remove an assignment, click the X on the employee chip within the job's drop zone. Every assignment or removal can be undone using the Undo button in the header (up to 5 steps).

Use the Settings tab to add or edit employees, manage skills, and rename crew groups. All changes reflect immediately across Schedule, Jobs, and Employees tabs.

Understanding the Outputs

Skill Gap: When employees assigned to a job do not collectively cover all required skills, a yellow "Skill gap" badge appears on that job row. Assignments are not blocked; this is a planning flag only.

Overtime Calculation: Overtime is calculated per week. Any hours worked over 40 in a single calendar week are paid at 1.5x the employee's hourly rate. The Employees tab shows Week 1 and Week 2 hours separately.

Labor Cost %: The day summary bar shows total labor cost for assigned employees as a percentage of total scheduled revenue for that day. Each assigned employee is assumed to work 8 hours per assigned job.

Job Status: Unassigned means no employees are on the job. Partial means employees are assigned but a skill gap exists. Fully Staffed means all required skills are covered by the assigned crew.
